Sundays, 11AM ET / 8AM PT

Reliable Sources with Brian Stelter examines the media world: how journalists do their jobs and how media outlets affect the stories they cover.

Show Description

This week's show 

    Trump slips, Fiorina surges in new poll

    Political columnist Ana Marie Cox and Politico chief political correspondent Glenn Thrush weigh in on press treatment of Trump, Clinton and other candidates.

    Trump sowing doubt about Obama's citizenship; what should journalists do?

    When Donald Trump tries to sow doubt about President Obama's citizenship, how should media figures respond?Stelter speaks to Newsmax TV host Steve Malzberg.

    Biggest hit of fall TV season: GOP debates

    David Zurawik, The Baltimore Sun's TV critic, and David Folkenflik, NPR's media correspondent, analyze CNN's GOP debate and what the record viewership represents.

    Red news, blue news, and the Pope

    Paul Vallely, the author of "Pope Francis: Untying the Knots," and CNN anchor Chris Cuomo look at how Pope Francis is portrayed by the American press.

    Brian Williams returning to the airwaves this week

    David Zurawik, The Baltimore Sun's TV critic, and David Folkenflik, NPR's media correspondent, discuss Brian Williams' upcoming debut on MSNBC.

    Brian Stelter

    Host
    Brian Stelter is the host of "Reliable Sources" and the senior media correspondent for CNN Worldwide.

    Arwa Damon on refugee crisis: "We are their only voice"

    Dick Cavett reviews Stephen Colbert's debut

    Brian Stelter speaks with Matt Welch, the editor of Reason magazine, about the recent DOJ subpoena regarding web comments that rocked the publication.
