Summary[edit]

DescriptionLloyd Fuller Diary, November 5-6, 1942 (14937860286).jpg

"November 5, 1942 Cleaned guns this morning. At 11:30 Tojo sent his bombers over the field. They dropped their bombs between the airfield & our 2nd lines. Because of the clouds, our fighters failed to make contact. Antiaircraft shot down 4 bombers, U.S.S. Saratoga knocked down 18 of 37 bombers, 15 planes got away. The new fellows got a taste of bombs today. Horne & I went swimming in the Lunga River this afternoon-!

November 6, 1942 Not much doing today. Oiled guns this morning. Laid around all day as #50 was not flying much. It rained like cats & dogs this afternoon. Maytag Charlie cruised around for two hours, beginning at midnight, but dropped no bombs. He dropped 2 flares. Not much sleep last night."

From the Lloyd D. Fuller Collection (COLL/4932), Marine Corps Archives & Special Collections
