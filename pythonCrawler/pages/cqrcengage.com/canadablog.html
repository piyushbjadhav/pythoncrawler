ADEA Canadian Blog

  • One-third of Ontario Workers Lack Medical/Dental Benefits, Study Says

    American Dental Education Association

    Many low-income workers in Ontario, Canada do not receive employer-provided medical or dental benefits, according to a study by the Wellesley Institute, a health policy think tank in Toronto.

  • Canadian White Paper Advocates Greater Primary Care Role for Dental Hygienists

    American Dental Education Association

    A white paper recently released by the College of Dental Hygienists of Nova Scotia (CDHNS) argues for fully integrating dental hygienists into Nova Scotia’s universally accessible primary care system. According to the authors, oral health inequities persist across Canada, and treatment, rather than prevention, is the focus of most Canadian dental practices. The paper asserts that dental hygienists possess the knowledge and competencies to play a vital role in promoting oral health and preventing oral disease in community-based settings. 

  • Canada Makes Changes to the Smoke-Free Ontario Act

    American Dental Education Association

    Ontario dentists are applauding the province’s new smoking ban, which takes effect on January 1, 2015. Changes to the Smoke-Free Ontario Act brought about the smoking ban. The Ontario Dental Association (ODA) is praising the ban on smoking in bar and restaurant patios, playgrounds and public sport fields. In addition, the sale of tobacco on college and university campuses will be outlawed.
