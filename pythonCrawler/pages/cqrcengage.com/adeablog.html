ADEA State Blog

  • Montana House Passes Bill to Expand Medicaid

    American Dental Education Association

    The Montana House approved, by a 54-46 margin, a bill to expand the state’s Medicaid program. The approved bill, SB 405, requires enrollees to pay premiums and co-pays as well as participate in job assessment programs. The bill now returns to the Senate for approval of the single amendment added by the House. Following the likely approval of the amendment, the bill will be sent to the governor.

  • New Mexico Introduces Bill to Increase the Number of Dental Students in the Western Interstate Commission for Higher Education

    American Dental Education Association

    Sen. Michael Padilla (D-NM) has introduced S.B. 35. The bill appropriates $400,000 from the general fund to the Higher Education Department (HED) to increase the number of students who may participate in the Western Interstate Commission for Higher Education (WICHE). The appropriation of $400,000 contained in S.B. 35 is a recurring expense to the general fund. The bill, as introduced, would nearly fund an additional four dental students for their four years of dental school. Currently, the state supports nearly 55 dental students per year, adding an average dental school cohort of 14 students annually. The total cost of the dental scholarships for FY15 is nearly $1.4 million. If S.B. 35 is enacted, HED could support nearly 60 students in FY16, growing the annual cohort to 18 students for nearly $1.5 million. The department estimates that tuition and fees rise an average of 3.5% annually, increasing the estimated cost of the loan-for-service program to $1.7 million in FY19.

  • Georgia and New Jersey Ask Congress to Act on Graduate Medical Education

    American Dental Education Association

    In February, H.R. 302 and S.R. 84 were introduced in the Georgia General Assembly. Both resolutions urge Congress to pass legislation increasing federal support for graduate medical education.
