Higher Education Act (HEA)

The Higher Education Act (HEA) is due for reauthorization. In preparation during the last Congress the committees in the House and Senate conducted hearings on various legislative topics including student financial aid, accreditation and measuring an institution’s success in serving students.

Gainful Employment

Since the Higher Education Act was first passed in 1965, it has included a version of the clause “prepare students for gainful employment in a recognized occupation.” Federal student aid requirements mandate that career education programs abide by this provision to receive loan funds disbursed under Title IV of the act.

North Carolina Board of Dental Examiners v. FTC

On February 25, the U.S. Supreme Court of the United States (Court) published its opinion in North Carolina Board of Dental Examiners v. Federal Trade Commission. The Court found the Board violated federal antitrust laws by issuing cease-and-desist letters to non-dentists who provided teeth-whitening services in North Carolina.

Read More

King v. Burwell

On June 25, 2015, the Supreme Court of the United States (Court) in a 6-3 decision handed down a ruling in King v. Burwell (King). The case centered on four words in the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act (ACA), “established by the state.” The Court sided with the Obama Administration, affirming that tax credits are available to individuals in both state and federally established exchanges.
